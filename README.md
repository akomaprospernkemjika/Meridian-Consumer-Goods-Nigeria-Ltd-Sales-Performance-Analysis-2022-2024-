## 📌 Project Overview

This analysis simulates a real-world FMCG sales operations dataset for 
**Meridian Consumer Goods Nigeria Ltd** — a fictional consumer goods distributor 
operating across 8 Nigerian states from 2022 to 2024.

The dataset was intentionally generated with real-world data quality issues 
including inconsistent formatting, duplicate records, missing values, text in 
number fields, negative quantities, and multiple date formats — reflecting the 
kind of messy data analysts encounter daily in Nigerian businesses.

---

## 🧹 Data Cleaning Performed

The raw dataset contained serious quality issues that required extensive cleaning:

| Problem Found | How I Fixed It |
|---------------|----------------|
| 7 different date formats mixed | Standardized to DD MM YYYY using MID formula |
| Inconsistent product names (MILO, milo, Milo ) | Standardized using PROPER and TRIM |
| Misspelled cities (Lagoss, Portharcourt, Ibadann) | Fixed using Find & Replace |
| Mixed category names (household, HOUSEHOLD, House Hold) | Standardized to proper case |
| Negative and zero units sold | Flagged with — for transparency |
| Text in price columns (TBD, unknown, five hundred) | Identified and flagged |
| ~5,000 duplicate rows scattered throughout | Removed using Remove Duplicates |
| Wrong revenue figures not matching Units × Price | Recalculated using correct formula |
| Missing Transaction IDs | Identified and flagged |
| Inconsistent sales rep names | Standardized to Proper Case |

## 🔍 Key Findings

### 📈 Year on Year Profit Growth

| Year | Total Profit | Growth |
|------|-------------|--------|
| 2022 | ₦8,777,126,795 | Baseline |
| 2023 | ₦8,797,841,883 | +₦20.7M (+0.24%) |
| 2024 | ₦8,851,548,168 | +₦53.7M (+0.61%) |

> 📌 Profit grew consistently every year with 2024 recording the strongest 
> performance. Growth accelerated from 0.24% to 0.61% — a positive trajectory 
> that suggests improving operational efficiency.

## 💡 Business Recommendations

**1. Double down on Lagos** — it is clearly the strongest market. More 
distribution points and marketing investment here will compound returns.

**2. Urgently investigate Abuja** — Nigeria's capital underperforming by 20.9% 
is a major red flag. Deploy additional sales reps and review distribution strategy.

**3. Reward top sales reps** — Fatima Bello, Chukwuemeka Obi and Ngozi Okonkwo 
are driving serious volume. Retention incentives will protect this performance.

**4. Accelerate 2024 momentum** — profit growth doubled from 0.24% to 0.61%. 
Identify exactly what changed in 2024 and scale it aggressively into 2025.

**5. Fix data collection processes** — the volume of missing prices, duplicate 
records and inconsistent formatting suggests the sales data entry process needs 
standardization across all regions.

## 🔄 How This Helps Real FMCG Companies

Any Nigerian FMCG company — whether a distributor, manufacturer, or retailer — 
faces these exact challenges daily:

- 📋 **Messy sales data** collected across multiple regions and reps
- 🌍 **Regional performance gaps** that are hard to spot without analysis
- 👥 **Sales rep accountability** — who is actually driving volume?
- 📈 **Year on year tracking** — are we actually growing or just busy?

This analysis provides a **replicable framework** that FMCG businesses can adapt 
to their own sales data to answer these exact questions — without expensive 
business intelligence software.
